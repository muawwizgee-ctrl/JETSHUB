DESCRIPTION

ABOUT ITS NAME

The Ka-52 is known as the Alligator. The name reflects its powerful and aggressive combat role. It was developed as an advanced attack helicopter. The helicopter is part of the Kamov aircraft family. Its name is recognized among military aviation enthusiasts. The Alligator symbolizes strength and battlefield capability. Today it is one of Russia's most advanced helicopters.

OVERVIEW

The Ka-52 is a twin-seat attack helicopter developed by Russia. It is designed for reconnaissance and combat missions. The helicopter combines firepower with advanced technology. It can operate day and night in various conditions. The aircraft supports ground forces on the battlefield. Its modern systems improve mission effectiveness. It remains an important part of Russian military aviation.

DEVELOPMENT HISTORY

The Ka-52 was developed from the Ka-50 Black Shark. Development began during the 1990s. The first flight took place in 1997. Engineers added a second crew member for improved operations. The helicopter entered military service in the 2010s. Several improvements were made during development. It became one of Russia's leading attack helicopters.

DESIGN FEATURES

The Ka-52 uses a unique coaxial rotor system. It does not require a traditional tail rotor. This design improves maneuverability and stability. The helicopter has armored protection for crew safety. Advanced sensors are mounted around the aircraft. Its cockpit is designed for efficient combat operations. The design makes the Ka-52 easily recognizable.

PERFORMANCE

The Ka-52 can reach speeds of around 300 km/h. It has excellent agility during combat maneuvers. The helicopter performs well in difficult environments. Its engines provide strong power and reliability. The aircraft can operate over long distances. It performs effectively during battlefield missions. Its performance ranks among the best attack helicopters.

ARMAMENT

The Ka-52 carries a powerful 30mm cannon. It can launch guided anti-tank missiles. Rocket pods provide additional firepower. The helicopter can carry air-to-air missiles. Different weapon combinations are available for missions. Its weapons are effective against various targets. The Ka-52 is heavily armed for combat operations.

TECHNOLOGY

The Ka-52 features advanced avionics systems. Modern sensors help locate and track targets. Digital displays improve pilot awareness. Night vision capability supports operations after dark. Communication systems enhance battlefield coordination. Electronic warfare systems improve survivability. Its technology increases overall combat effectiveness.

OPERATORS

Russia is the primary operator of the Ka-52. The helicopter serves in Russian military aviation units. Export versions have been offered internationally. Several countries have shown interest in the aircraft. It is used for attack and reconnaissance missions. The helicopter continues to attract global attention. Its operator base may expand in the future.

COMBAT HISTORY

The Ka-52 has participated in modern military operations. It has been used in combat support missions. The helicopter demonstrated its attack capabilities in real conflicts. Its advanced weapons proved effective on the battlefield. The aircraft has supported ground forces during operations. Combat experience has influenced future upgrades. Its service history continues to grow.

VARIANTS

Several versions of the Ka-52 have been developed. The standard Ka-52 serves land-based forces. The Ka-52K variant was designed for naval operations. Export models include customized equipment. Different versions support different mission requirements. Upgrades continue to improve performance and systems. The helicopter family remains highly capable.

INTERESTING FACTS

The Ka-52 uses two counter-rotating rotors. It is one of the few helicopters without a tail rotor. The aircraft evolved from the Ka-50 Black Shark. Its design is unique among attack helicopters. The Ka-52 is featured in military exhibitions worldwide. Many aviation enthusiasts admire its appearance. It is one of Russia's most recognizable helicopters.

SPECIFICATIONS

The Ka-52 is powered by two turboshaft engines. It has a crew of two members. Maximum speed is approximately 300 km/h. The helicopter carries missiles, rockets, and a cannon. Its range supports long combat missions. The aircraft is designed for attack operations. It remains a modern and capable combat helicopter.
