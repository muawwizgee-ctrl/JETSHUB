DESCRIPTION

ABOUT ITS NAME

The An-124 is officially known as the Ruslan. The name comes from a legendary hero in Eastern European folklore. It reflects the aircraft's enormous size and strength. The aircraft was designed for heavy strategic airlift missions. Its name became famous throughout the aviation world. The Ruslan is one of the largest transport aircraft ever built. It remains an icon of heavy cargo aviation.

OVERVIEW

The Antonov An-124 is a strategic heavy transport aircraft. It was developed to move oversized cargo and military equipment. The aircraft can transport tanks, helicopters, and industrial machinery. Its enormous cargo hold allows exceptional carrying capability. The An-124 is used for both military and civilian missions. It has operated around the world for decades. The aircraft remains one of the largest cargo planes ever produced.

DEVELOPMENT HISTORY

The An-124 was developed by the Antonov Design Bureau. Work on the aircraft began during the 1970s. The first flight took place in 1982. It entered service during the mid-1980s. Engineers focused on payload capacity and long-range performance. The aircraft quickly gained international recognition. It became a major achievement in transport aviation.

DESIGN FEATURES

The An-124 features a massive high-wing design. Four powerful turbofan engines provide strong performance. Its cargo bay is among the largest ever built. The aircraft has front and rear cargo loading capability. Heavy-duty landing gear supports large payloads. Its design allows efficient loading operations. The aircraft was created for demanding transport missions.
