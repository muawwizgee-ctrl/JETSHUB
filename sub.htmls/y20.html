ABOUT ITS NAME

The Y-20 is a Chinese strategic transport aircraft. It was developed by Xi'an Aircraft Industrial Corporation. The designation Y stands for transport aircraft in Chinese service. The aircraft was designed to strengthen China's airlift capability. Its name is now well known in military aviation circles. The Y-20 represents a major achievement for Chinese aerospace. Today it serves as China's primary heavy transport aircraft.

OVERVIEW

The Xi'an Y-20 is a large military transport aircraft. It is designed for strategic airlift and logistics missions. The aircraft carries troops, vehicles, and heavy cargo. Its large cargo hold allows transport of oversized equipment. The Y-20 supports military and humanitarian operations. It plays a key role in rapid deployment missions. The aircraft continues expanding China's global airlift capability.

DEVELOPMENT HISTORY

The Y-20 was developed to meet China's strategic transport needs. Development began during the early 2000s. The aircraft made its first flight in 2013. It entered operational service a few years later. Engineers focused on payload capacity and long-range performance. Modern technology was integrated throughout the design. The Y-20 became one of China's most important military aircraft.
